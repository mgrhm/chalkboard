Prior Learning

Rainforest topic (ongoing). Whole-class discussion with rainforest music in background) of impressive and relevant adjectives for each of the senses.

Creative writing piece "a walk in the rainforest". Adjective-filled paragraphs for sight, sounds, smell, touch, and taste.

Learning Intention(s)

  • Adapt previous creative writing in jotter about senses in the rain forest to:
  • make a short online book, or;
  • poem(s), or;
  • an interactive computer text story game, or;
  • a board game.

Success Criteria

  • Throughout the writing process, I can check that my writing makes sense and meets its purpose. LIT 2-23a
  • Uses paragraphs to separate thoughts and ideas
  • Uses a range of punctuation, for example, capital letters, full stops, commas, inverted commas (speech marks), exclamation marks, question marks and/or apostrophes. Punctuation is mainly accurate.

Curricular Links (Scotland's Curriculum for Excellence)

  • I consider the impact that layout and presentation will have and can combine lettering, graphics and other features to engage my reader. LIT 2-24a
  • I can convey information, describe events, explain processes or combine ideas in different ways. LIT 2-28a

Learning activities and use of teaching time:

Introduction (5-10 mins)

Explain that the previous writing on five senses in the rainforest will be adapted to another media. Allow the children to form into groups based on their choice of story book, poem book, video game or board game.

Main Section (1-2 hours per lesson)

  1. Children briefly study the guide sheets which include specific instructions for each choice.
  2. The children should review their writing and make corrections or expand on their thoughts in their jotters before committing to the new media.
  3. Children work on their own to develop their deliverable, while helping each other out and giving each other feedback at 10-15 minute intervals.

Plenary (5-10 mins)

  1. At least one volunteer from each group presents their work so far.
  2. Feedback/ideas given by other children for author’s consideration.

Assessment of learning and children's next steps:

During lesson

  • Self, peer and teacher checks and corrections throughout the creative process at 10-15 min intervals.
  • Uses paragraphs to separate thoughts and ideas
  • Children able to explain their rationale for where new paragraphs are made? What is the link?
  • Uses a range of punctuation, for example, capital letters, full stops, commas, inverted commas (speech marks), exclamation marks, question marks and/or apostrophes. Punctuation is mainly accurate.

Next steps

Offer opportunities for children to think of even more creative applications and media for their Rainforest topic. Allow children to write reviews of the other children’s work to develop critical thinking and persuasion skills.
